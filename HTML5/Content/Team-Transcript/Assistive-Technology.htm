Assistive Technology and Transcripts

Transcripts created for and from videos can be used to create closed captions, or at least subtitles.

  • Closed captions and subtitles are especially useful for those with hearing disabilities, as they “render any audio information in text form to the user” (“Transcript assistive technology”).

  • For those with visual impairments, the screen of a device can be magnified so they can see the text more easily; if a person is completely blind, they can benefit from effective audio that narrates the transcript (“Transcript assistive technology”).

  • Screen readers can also read transcripts for people who might have difficulty reading or following along with transcripts/subtitles/closed captions.
